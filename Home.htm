Heart

Circulatory System

Every cell in our body takes in oxygen and nutrients, and releases carbon dioxide. Our blood sends these essential nutrients and oxygen through the blood vessels, and along the way, picks up the waste product of carbon dioxide. In other words, blood is tasked with providing cells with food, and taking away waste products. This maintenance of the input and output, the blood flow, is necessary for cells to function.

Ultimately, the heart controls this blood flow. With every pump, the heart sends blood through the whole body to support the cells. This flow is known as Systemic Flow; the blood movement between the heart and body.

After the heart sends out blood to the body cells, the blood will lack oxygen and nutrients, having been given out to the cells. To resupply the blood for the next Systemic circulation, it is sent through the lungs, that will replenish the blood's oxygen supply. The process of going through the lungs is known as the Pulmatory Flow.
