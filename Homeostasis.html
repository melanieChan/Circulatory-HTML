Homeostasis

The heart helps the body maintain homeostasis through its control over the heart rate. Homeostasis is defined as the stability of a state or condition. To maintain stability when body's environment changes, the cardiovascular system will regulate the heart rate based on the specific circumstance of the body. For example, when the body exercises, all the cells in the body are working harder, and they would need more oxygen to support themselves. The heart would beat faster, releasing more blood into the body to support the cells. By increasing the heart rate, homeostasis is maintained when the heart keeps up with the delivery of oxygen to cells that would balance the increased cellular output of carbon dioxide.
