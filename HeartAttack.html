Heart Attack

A heart attack is caused by clogging of the coronary arteries. These arteries are the paths to provide oxygen and nutrients to the heart muscle cells. They become obstructed when plaques; the buildup of fats, calcium, proteins, and dead white blood cells; block the arteries and limit the blood flow. When the surface of the plaque ruptures, it forms a blood clot. If the clot grows and blocks off the artery completely, the cells sustained by that artery would die of deprivation of oxygen, and this situation results the permanent death of heart cells, or a heart attack.
