Function & Purpose

The heart, a major organ which plays a prominent role in the circulatory system, is responsible for pumping blood throughout the body. Specifically, the Right and Left Ventricles are the chambers that are able to pump blood out. These two chambers, along with two others, are all part of the blood flow paths within the heart that travel for replenishing.

First, the blood from the previous circulation comes in from the Superior and Inferior Vena Cavas.
Then it enters the Right Atrium(1st chamber), and passes through the Tricuspid Valve into the Right Ventricle(2nd chamber).
From the Right Ventricle, it continues up the Pulmonary Arteries, that would go into the lungs for resupply, and come back into the heart from the Pulmonary Veins.
The Pulmonary Veins lead to the Left Atrium(3rd chamber), and from there it passes down through the Mitral Valve into the Left Ventricle.
Ready to leave the heart, it finally passes through the Aortic Valve into the Aorta, which has three arteries leading out to the rest of the body.
